Teaching Labor

an Adelphi University MOOC

"One percent of the nation owns a third of the wealth. The rest of the wealth is distributed in such a way as to turn those in the 99 percent against one another: small property owners against the propertyless, black against white, native-born against foreign-born, intellectuals and professionals against the uneducated and the unskilled."

Howard Zinn, A People's History of the United States

In the Fall of 2014, we will launch a new Massive Open Online Course (MOOC) called Teaching Labor, where a group of teachers, students, activists, and interested individuals will come together to study the history of the working class and labor movement, and develop methods for incorporating these ideas into our schools and clasrooms.

Quick Facts:

  • the course will be offered free of charge
  • anyone with a computer and internet connection can sign up
  • we encourage teachers and labor activists to join us

About the instructors

Rob Linne teaches in the adolescent education program at Adelphi University. His research and writing focuses on the history of working people's struggle in the United States. He is the co-author of The New York City Triangle Factory Fire.

Matt Curinga teaches in the educational technology program at Adelphi University. His research into the politics of software design and digital media draws in his background as a computer programmer.
